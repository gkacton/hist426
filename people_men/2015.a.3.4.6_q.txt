A launch title for this system featured the first appearance of Professor E. Gadd and allowed the player to stun enemies with a flashlight before sucking them up with the Poltergust 3000. During development, this system was codenamed  “Dolphin”. After the failure of the Dreamcast, Sega released Super Monkey Ball for this console. This system used a popular wireless controller called the  WaveBird, though it was criticized for its proprietary miniDVD format. Its exclusive games include Luigi’s Mansion and Super Smash Bros.
