A depiction of this place in the parekklesion  of the Chora  Church features Jesus holding the hands of Adam and Eve. This place is commonly depicted as a "mouth" in manuscript paintings, which often feature depictions of its  “harrowing.” The New Testament repeatedly refers to its inhabitants “weeping and gnashing” their  teeth. This place is depicted in the right-hand panel of Bosch’s Garden of Earthly Delights and is usually on the right in paintings of the Last Judgement.
