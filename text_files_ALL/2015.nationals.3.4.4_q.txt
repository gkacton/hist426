The "ring and girdle motif" is carved into some of these objects. The Season 5 expedition revealed a buried underground component to many of these objects, whose largest examples include "El Gigante" and "Paro." Much of the material for these artworks came from mining tuff from the Rano (+) Raraku crater. A subset of these 887 objects displays topknots called pukao. Centuries before (*) Chilean colonists arrived, these artworks were dragged into place on rolling logs by the Rapa Nui people.
