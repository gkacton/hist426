This man wrote against slavery in one city's Gazette under aliases like "Vindex" and “Candidus.” Following the arrival of the Dartmouth this man and James Otis distributed the "circular letter" to organize a meeting at Faneuil [[FAN-yoo-il]] Hall. Many years after his best known action, this man succeeded John Hancock to become the fourth governor of Massachusetts. For ten points, name this Founding Father and leader of the Sons of Liberty who orchestrated the Boston Tea Party.
