Often incorporating aspects of the cult of Serapis [[seh-RAP-iss]], this deity names a "Mystery" practiced in the Greco-Roman world which guaranteed practitioners a place in the Fortunate Isles. An aspect of this goddess related to "Pharos" [[FAY-rohss]] was worshipped by sailor's hoping to have favorable seas. Scholars have speculated that images of this goddess with Horus on her lap inspired iconography of Mary with an infant Christ.
