A faction of this tribe were led by the only Native American to reach the rank of general during the U.S. Civil War, Stand Watie, while fighting for the Confederacy. 19th century writer John Howard Payne wrote an oral historiography of this people after being  invited as a guest of Chief John Ross. A syllabary was created for this people's language by the polymath  Sequoyah. For ten points, name these indigenous people of the American South, the largest of the "Five Civilized" tribes who experienced the "Trail of Tears."
